I’m a postdoctoral research fellow at Purdue University, working with Prof. Woongkul Matt Lee. Before joining Purdue, I worked as a Postdoctoral Research Fellow with the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering, North Carolina State University, advised by Prof. Hao Su. I received my Ph.D. from Nanyang Technological University (NTU) in 2024, where I was advised by Prof. Christopher H. T. Lee.

My research interests lie in the intersection of foundation electric machine drive and wearable robotics at the system level. My long-term vision is to develop human-centered electro-mechanical systems that seamlessly integrate with people and society, enabling more sustainable transportation and healthier, more independent living. My research is built upon two synergistic pillars: sustainable electric machine drive technologies for vehicle electrification and intelligent wearable robotic systems for human augmentation and rehabilitation.

I am a passionate tennis player whose personal best is NTRP 3.5. I keep pushing my limits, improving from NTRP 2.0 in 2022 to NTRP 3.0 in 2024, and reaching NTRP 3.5 in 2025. I also enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities, including pickleball, badminton, soccer, table tennis, and running.
